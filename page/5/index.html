Ashley 1.7.1 released

Jan 10, 2016 · 1 minute read · Comments
Games developmentProjects

There’s a new Ashley release, check the changes for 1.7.1 below:

  • API addition: expose IntervalSystem interval value (read-only).
  • Bug fix: fixed pending entity operations not being processed in the right order.
  • Bug fix: fixed adding component in empty family EntityListener causing wrong EntityListener calls.
  • Bug fix: fixed some entity operations not being performed under special circumstances.
  • Internals: split Engine internals into loosely coupled, single responsibility smaller classes.
  • Update: uses Libgdx 1.8.0.

To use it, change your dependency to com.badlogicgames.gdx:ashley:1.7.1. The new nightly dependency is com.badlogicgames.gdx:ashley:1.7.2-SNAPSHOT.

It’s really motivating to see how many people are using Ashley in their projects, including for the #LibGDXJAM!

LibGDXJAM update #3

Jan 10, 2016 · 1 minute read · Comments
Games developmentProjects

Oh my God, enter panic mode, just 8 days left until the #LibGDXJAM deadline! We’ve been hard at work, especially @DrillerDev who’s pushed the character design and animation.

Look at this bad boy for instance, better not to mess with his alien gun.

Or the beholder powered alien surveillance.

Please, ignore the placeholder environment art, it’s the textures from the Overlap2D example project. Our chubby astronaut can run, crouch, jump and push boxes around. We’ve also worked on a nice look-ahead camera target tracking system. That way the player knows where the character is going and anticipate dangers.

One of the ways to avoid CCTV cameras is to prone behind a crate and push it whilst remaining hidden.

We"re now focusing on enemy behaviours and level design.

LibGDXJAM New Year's update

Jan 1, 2016 · 1 minute read · Comments
Games developmentProjects

We"re halfway through the #libGDXJAM and some work has been done between Christmas and New Year’s eve.

Here’s a few updates.

  • @DrillerDev as joined us as an artist and has already produced some awesome concepts.
  • Alberto and I have been working on an Overlap2D scene loader and it’s pretty much working supporting a bunch of things: composites, textures, lights, physics and particles.
  • We have a somewhat buggy physics based platformer movement system.
  • There are cameras you have to stay away from!

We really need to focus on gameplay now, hopefully we"ll have something playable by the deadline. Make sure to follow the Twitter feed for the jam. Gotta say that I"m shocked to see how much coolness people have managed to put together in such a short amount of time.
